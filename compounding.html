Eighth Wonder of the World

Albert Einstein allegedly once said "Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world." Compound growth is a great ally to an investor. There is the old math problem which asks you whether you would take $1 million dollars up front or a penny that will double everyday until the end of 30 days. Due to compound growth, you should take the penny. After 20 days, the balance is only $5242. At the end of 30 days, you would have over $10 million. As you can see, it is imperative to start early though because most of that growth occurs towards the end. The following table illustrates the multiplier effect of annual compounding growth of 10% on a monthly contribution amount until age 65.

As you can see from the table, the earliest years give you the greatest multiplier on your contributions and requires a much less monthly contribution to reach the goal amount. Every 10 years you wait would require an approximately 3X contribution amount to reach the same final amount.

Comparison of Two Savers
Start Saving at Age Stop Saving at Age Monthly Contribution ($) Balance at Age 65 ($)
22 35 1000 5,548,039
35 65 1000 2,260,488

The person who started saving at age 22 and stopped at age 35 contributed a lifetime amount of $156,000 over 13 years while the person who started at age 35 and stopped at age 65 contributed a lifetime amount of $408,000 over 34 years. However, the person who started early finished at age 65 with a balance almost 2.5X of the person who started later while contributing almost 1/3 the amount. That is the power of compound growth.
